Straight Key & Sideswiper

Two compact printable manual keys built for hands-on CW. The straight key uses a traditional up-and-down action, while the sideswiper alternates lateral contact for a different sending rhythm. Both designs are intended to be usable, repeatable, and repairable.

The straight key evolved from a single print-in-place prototype into a serviceable two-print design. The finished hardware uses accessible contact screws and a 3.5 mm socket for a removable cable.

Prototype lesson

Print-in-place proved the idea

The one-piece version reduced assembly and made the first prototype approachable. It also buried the contact screws under the lever, making the key harder to inspect, adjust, and repair.

Revision direction

Two parts made it serviceable

The updated base and lever/top split exposes the contact area. Indexing pins align the pieces, one fastener clamps them together, and the jack mount supports removable tip-to-sleeve key wiring.

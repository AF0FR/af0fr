Morse Straight Key

A compact printable straight key designed to be usable, repeatable, and repairable. The first version was a single print-in-place part that proved the lever geometry. The later revision moved to a two-print design so wire terminations and contact screws are easier to reach.

The final design accommodates a 3.5 mm socket for a removable cable, with M4 screws and the jack handling the electrical connection.

Prototype lesson

Print-in-place proved the idea

The one-piece version reduced assembly and made the first prototype approachable. It also buried the contact screws under the lever, making the key harder to inspect, adjust, and repair.

Revision direction

Two parts made it serviceable

The updated base and lever/top split exposes the contact area. Indexing pins align the pieces, one fastener clamps them together, and the jack mount supports removable tip-to-sleeve key wiring.
